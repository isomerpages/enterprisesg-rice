<p>Circular No. 9
</p>
<p>29 July 2013
</p>
<p>All holders of License to import Parboiled Rice, Basmati Rice and Ponni Rice
</p>
<p><strong>RICE STOCKPILE SCHEME FOR PARBOILED RICE, BASMATI RICE AND PONNI RICE</strong>
</p>
<p>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; This Circular only applies to Rice Stockpile Scheme for Parboiled Rice, Basmati Rice and Ponni Rice. Unless otherwise specified,&nbsp;<em>“P-RSS licensees”</em>&nbsp;in this Circular refer to holders of license to conduct import&nbsp;<em>‘Parboiled Rice, Basmati Rice and Ponni</em>&nbsp;Rice’ only. For licensees who import both&nbsp;<em>‘White rice’</em>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<em>‘Parboiled Rice, Basmati Rice and Ponni Rice’</em>, please refer to a separate circular (Circular No. 10) for change in regulations. This set of policy changes does not apply to them.
</p>
<p>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<strong><em>With effect from 1 Jan 2014</em></strong>, the following new requirements will be applicable to all P-RSS Licensees: &nbsp;
</p>
<p>(1)&nbsp;&nbsp;New Prescribed Stockpile Quantity (PSQ) Computation
</p>
<p>There will be a&nbsp;<em>minimum PSQ of 5 MT</em>&nbsp;imposed on all P-RSS licensees. P-RSS licensees who wish to continue with their licenses to import&nbsp;<em>‘</em>Parboiled rice, Basmati Rice and Ponni Rice’<em>&nbsp;</em>shall commit to maintenance of a minimum PSQ of 5 MT at all times, even if there is no import activity.
</p>
<p>Each Calendar year will be divided into&nbsp;<strong><em>two 6-month periods</em></strong>&nbsp;as follows:

<p>PSQ for each period will be computed based on twice the average import quantity for the preceding period (of 6 months) or 5 MT, whichever is higher.
</p>
<p>Transition arrangement&nbsp;<em>only&nbsp;</em>for the period&nbsp;<em>‘January 2014 to June 2014’</em>
</p>
<p>For the period&nbsp;<em>‘January 2014 to June 2014’</em>, PSQ will be computed based on the sum of actual imports from June 2013 to November 2013 (a total of 6 months). Imports in December 2013 will not be included in the calculation in order to facilitate the building up of required PSQ. P-RSS licensees will be informed on the exact PSQ for the period&nbsp;<em>‘January 2014 to June’</em>&nbsp;by 6 December 2013 and licensees have until 31<sup>st</sup>&nbsp;Jan 2014 to meet the PSQ requirement.
</p>
<p>&nbsp;
</p>
<p>(2)&nbsp;&nbsp;Maximum Import Quantity (hereinafter referred to as “Max IQ”)
</p>
<p>The total import quantity allowed to be imported in a 6-month period, known as&nbsp;<em>‘Maximum Import Quantity’ (Max IQ)</em>, will be capped at thrice the PSQ. However, there is no requirement on minimum import quantity. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</p>
<p>PSQ and Max IQ will be adjusted in the months of January and July each year. IE Singapore will inform all P-RSS Licensees of their PSQ and Max IQ for the next period by the first week of January and July each year.
</p>
<p>Only one upward adjustment in Max IQ, with a corresponding adjustment in PSQ, will be allowed during a 6-month period at the P-RSS Licensee’s request. No downward adjustment will be permitted. &nbsp;&nbsp;
</p>

</p>

</p>

</p>
<p>IE Singapore will acknowledge your reply within 2 working days for (a) and instantaneously for (b). Please inform us if you do not receive an acknowledgement.

</p>

<p>Suppose on&nbsp;<em>1 October 201Y</em>&nbsp;the importer requests for Maximum Import Quantity to be increased from 150MT to 240 MT so that he may import an additional 90 MT during the period&nbsp;<em>“Jul – Dec 201Y”.&nbsp;</em>Once the request is approved, the<em>&nbsp;</em>importer has to top up the difference in stockpile.
</p>
<p>The new stockpile = 240/3 = 80MT
</p>
<p>Therefore, the importer has to top up 30MT in stockpile for him to be allowed a total import of 240MT in the period.
</p>
<p>Note: The total actual import quantity in the preceding 6-month period will be used for computation of PSQ for the next 6-month period. That is, say, despite requesting for the Maximum Import Quantity to be raised to 240 MT, the importer only managed to import 210MT by Dec 201Y, the PSQ for the next period “Jan-201Z to June-201Z” (201Z is the year after 201Y) will be computed based on 210MT (the actual import quantity). If this happens, the new PSQ for the next period = 210/3 = 70MT. The importer may withdraw the excess 10MT in Jan 201Z since it has stockpiled 80MT.
</p>

<p><strong>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong><strong>Why is there a change in policy?</strong>
</p>
<p>The revised policy seeks to improve efficiency for importers who currently need to stockpile whenever there are imports. An approach to a more consistent PSQ is therefore preferred.&nbsp;
</p>

<p><strong>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong><strong>If I did not import any quantity for the last 6-month period (say from January to June), what will be my PSQ for the next period (from July to Dec)?</strong>
</p>
<p>PSQ is based on 2 times average of the last 6-month period or 5MT, whichever is higher. Therefore the PSQ is 5MT.
</p>
<p><strong>4.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong><strong>Can I keep the license if I stockpile 5 MT and yet do not conduct any import activity?</strong>
</p>
<p>Yes.
</p>
<p><strong>5.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong><strong>Each of the white rice importers has a Monthly Import Quantity (MIQ) to fulfil. Is this applicable to importers of Parboiled rice, Basmati rice and Ponni rice under the revised Scheme?</strong>
</p>
<p>No, there is no MIQ for importers of Parboiled rice, Basmati rice and Ponni rice.
</p>
<p><strong>6.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong><strong>What will happen to my License if I did not send my Reply to IE Singapore by 15 September 2013?</strong>
</p>
<p>IE Singapore will deem all non-responses as decisions by P-RSS Licensees to opt out of the Scheme. Thus, if you do not reply by the stated date, your License will be terminated after 15 September 2013.
</p>
<p>7.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<strong>(Continue from Q6). If I choose to terminate my License, why should I take an additional step to reply since it will be terminated after 15 September 2013 if I do not respond anyway?&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;
</p>
<p>IE Singapore is offering a one-time free conversion of License for current P-RSS Licensees who opt to terminate their Licenses. The Import License may be converted to a Wholesale License, Import for re-export License or Import License for Non-stockpile Grade rice. This offer is only applicable to current P-RSS Licensees who indicated their intention to terminate and their interest for such conversion using the prescribed Reply Slip (as in Annex B). P-RSS Licensees who do not respond by the due date will not be able to enjoy this free conversion.
</p>
<p><strong>8.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong><strong>I am currently not a white rice importer. Now I would like to import both white rice and Parboiled, Basmati and Ponni rice. What should I do?</strong>
</p>
<p>You should write to IE Singapore. Once approved, you will be regulated under the Rice Stockpile Scheme for white rice and&nbsp;NOT&nbsp;this Scheme.
</p>
<p><strong>9.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong><strong>If my Maximum Import Quantity for a 6-month period is 50 MT but I only managed to import 30MT, can I roll over the remaining 20MT to the next period?</strong>
</p>
<p>No remaining quota for a period may be rolled over to the next period. The accumulated import quantity over a period will be refreshed at the beginning of a new period.
</p>
<p><strong>10.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong><strong>If I do not continue my license now, can I apply for the same license in&nbsp;<a target="blank" name="_GoBack"></a>future?</strong>
</p>
<p>Yes. However, if you apply for a stockpile grade import license immediately after you terminate your current license, your application will&nbsp;<em>NOT&nbsp;</em>be approved.&nbsp; You will be allowed to obtain a new stockpile grade import license only after 6 months from the date of your license termination. Nevertheless, this does not affect your application for non-stockpile grade rice import license, wholesale license, import for re-export license or license for manufacturing.
</p>
